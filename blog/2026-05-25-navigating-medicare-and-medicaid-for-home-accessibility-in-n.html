The Nuances of Medicare and Home Accessibility

First, let's talk about Medicare. This is the federal health insurance program primarily for people aged 65 or older, and certain younger people with disabilities. While it's a lifesaver for medical bills, hospital stays, and prescription drugs, it has very specific limitations when it comes to home modifications.

Generally speaking, Medicare Part A (Hospital Insurance) and Part B (Medical Insurance) do NOT cover home modifications like wheelchair ramps, grab bars, widening doorways, or stairlifts. Why not? Medicare typically classifies these as "home improvements" or "custodial care," not as direct medical treatment or Durable Medical Equipment (DME).

However, there are exceptions and important distinctions to understand:

1. Durable Medical Equipment (DME): Medicare does cover some DME if it's medically necessary and prescribed by a doctor. This includes items like hospital beds, oxygen equipment, wheelchairs (manual or power), and walkers. These are items that can be used repeatedly, serve a medical purpose, and are not useful to someone who isn't sick or injured.
