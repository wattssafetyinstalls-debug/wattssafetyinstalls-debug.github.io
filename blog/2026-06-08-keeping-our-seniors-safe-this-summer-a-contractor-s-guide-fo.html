Beating the Heat and Staying Hydrated: Summer Health & Home Prep

When you think of Nebraska summers, what comes to mind? Often, it's those beautiful, sunny days, but also the sometimes oppressive heat and humidity. These conditions can be particularly dangerous for seniors, who may not sense thirst as readily or regulate body temperature as efficiently.

1. Prioritize Hydration and Recognize Heat Stress: This isn't just about drinking water when you're thirsty. Encourage consistent fluid intake throughout the day – water, clear broths, and fruit juices are excellent. Avoid excessive caffeine or alcohol, which can lead to dehydration. Keep an eye out for signs of heat exhaustion or heatstroke: confusion, dizziness, heavy sweating, a rapid pulse, or a headache. If you or a loved one experiences these symptoms, seek medical attention immediately. Simple things like having water bottles easily accessible in every room can make a big difference.

2. Optimize Your Home for Cooling: Many homes in Northeast Nebraska, especially older ones, weren't built with modern central air conditioning in mind. If you have AC, make sure it's serviced and running efficiently. If not, strategic use of fans can help. Use ceiling fans to circulate air, and box fans to draw cooler air in during the evening and push hot air out during the day. Keep blinds and curtains closed on south and west-facing windows during the hottest parts of the day to block direct sunlight. Consider adding reflective window films or external awnings for older homes – small investments that can significantly reduce indoor temperatures and cut down on energy bills.

3. Smart Outdoor Activity: We all love spending time outdoors in June, but timing is key. Encourage outdoor activities during the cooler parts of the day – early mornings or late evenings. When going outside, wear lightweight, light-colored clothing, a wide-brimmed hat, and always use sunscreen. If you have an older deck or patio, now is a good time to check for loose boards or railings that could become hazards if you're spending more time outside.

Preventing Falls Indoors and Out: Navigating Summer Hazards

Falls are a leading cause of injury among seniors, and summer can introduce new risks. From wet surfaces to uneven ground, a little preventative maintenance can go a long way.
