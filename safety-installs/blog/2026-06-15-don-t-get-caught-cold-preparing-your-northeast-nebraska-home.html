Don't Get Caught Cold: Preparing Your Northeast Nebraska Home for Winter, Even in June!

It’s June, the sun is shining, and the fields around Norfolk, NE are green and growing. Most folks aren't thinking about heavy coats, icy winds, or the bone-chilling cold that defines a true Nebraska winter. But let me ask you this: how much did your heating bill sting last January? For many of us in Northeast Nebraska, those monthly statements are a stark reminder of just how hard our homes work to keep us warm. What if I told you that right now, in the glorious warmth of summer, is actually the absolute best time to start planning and executing home improvements that will slash those winter energy bills and keep your family cozy?

Why Summer is the Prime Time for Winterizing Your Home

You might think energy efficiency is a winter concern, but that's a bit like waiting until you're stranded in a blizzard to buy a snow shovel. Summer offers ideal conditions for many crucial upgrades. Temperatures are mild, making attic and crawl space work far more tolerable for installers. Materials like caulk and sealants cure properly without the risk of freezing or extreme humidity. Plus, contractors, including us at Watts Safety Installs, often have more flexible schedules before the rush of fall and winter repairs begins. Getting ahead now means you won't be scrambling for appointments when the first frost hits.

One of the simplest, yet most overlooked, areas for energy savings is air sealing. Think of your home as a giant sieve. Even if you have good insulation, if cold air can easily seep in through cracks and gaps, your furnace will work overtime.

Actionable Tip #1: DIY Air Sealing & Weatherstripping. Take advantage of a pleasant summer day to walk around your home's exterior and interior. Look for gaps around window frames, door frames, and where different building materials meet. Check electrical outlets and light switches on exterior walls – these are often hidden culprits for air leaks. A tube of good quality exterior caulk and some fresh weatherstripping for doors and windows can make a surprising difference. It's a low-cost, high-impact project you can tackle yourself. For larger, more persistent drafts, or if you suspect issues with your existing windows and doors, it might be time to consider professional upgrades.

Major Upgrades for Maximum Impact

While small DIY fixes are great, some improvements offer a much bigger return on investment, especially for older homes common in our region. These are the projects where professional expertise truly shines, transforming your home's energy performance.

The biggest culprit for heat loss in most homes is often inadequate insulation. Heat rises, and if your attic isn't properly insulated, you're essentially heating the great outdoors. I've been in countless attics in Norfolk, NE, and beyond where the insulation is either old, compressed, or simply insufficient for our harsh winters.

Insulation Upgrade: Consider adding blown-in cellulose or fiberglass insulation to your attic, bringing it up to modern R-values. If you have uninsulated walls or crawl spaces, spray foam insulation can be a game-changer, sealing and insulating simultaneously. These aren't just about adding warmth; they create a more consistent indoor temperature, eliminating cold spots and reducing the strain on your heating system.
