Summer Home Improvement: Making Your Northeast Nebraska Home a Safe, Cool Haven

It’s July, and here in Northeast Nebraska, the summer sun is in full swing. For many of us, this time of year brings back memories of long days spent outdoors, kids running through sprinklers, and the smell of barbecue wafting through the air. But for homeowners, July can also be a wake-up call. I recently visited a client in Norfolk, NE, who called me out for a seemingly minor deck repair. What we found, after a closer inspection, was a railing post that was severely rotted at its base, completely hidden by paint and years of neglect. It was a serious safety hazard, just waiting for someone to lean on it the wrong way. It’s a common story, and it highlights a crucial point: summer isn't just for enjoying your home; it's prime time for ensuring it's safe, efficient, and ready for whatever the unpredictable Nebraska weather throws our way.

Let's talk about some smart, actionable improvements you can make right now to enhance your home's safety, comfort, and long-term value.

Optimizing Outdoor Spaces for Safety and Enjoyment

Our decks, porches, and patios are extensions of our living spaces during the summer. But with all the foot traffic, barbecues, and general wear and tear, these areas need regular attention to remain safe. I’ve seen firsthand how quickly a small issue can become a major hazard.

Actionable Tip 1: Conduct a thorough deck and railing inspection. Don't just eyeball it. Get down on your hands and knees and really look. Check for loose boards, protruding nails, or screws. Pay particular attention to your railings – give them a good shake. Are they sturdy? Look for any signs of rot, especially where posts meet the deck surface or the ground. For wooden decks, inspect for splintering and consider a fresh sealant to protect against our intense sun and future moisture. If you have steps, ensure they are firmly attached and that any handrails are secure. Many older homes in our region might have decks that haven't been updated in decades, and materials can degrade over time. If you find anything concerning, don't put it off. We offer comprehensive safety installation services that cover everything from minor repairs to full safety overhauls, ensuring your outdoor living areas are ready for prime time.

Beyond decks, consider your fencing. A secure fence isn't just about privacy; it's vital for pet safety, child protection, and clearly defining your property lines. After a harsh winter and wet spring, posts can heave or rot, and panels can warp. Check your fence posts for stability and ensure gates latch securely.

Beating the Heat and Protecting Your Investment

While we love the sunny days, July in Northeast Nebraska can bring some serious heat and humidity. This is where your home’s envelope – its insulation, windows, and doors – becomes critical, not just for comfort but for protecting your wallet from high energy bills.

Actionable Tip 2: Assess your attic insulation. Go into your attic (carefully!) and take a look. Is the insulation evenly distributed? Does it look thin or compressed in spots? In our climate, aiming for an R-value of R-49 to R-60 is ideal for energy efficiency, especially with our scorching summers and brutal winters. If you can see your ceiling joists, you likely don't have enough. Adding insulation is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve your home's energy performance
